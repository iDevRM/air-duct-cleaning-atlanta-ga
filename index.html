What Is Air Duct Cleaning in Atlanta?

Air duct cleaning is the process of removing dust, pollen, pet dander, and debris that build up inside the metal or flex ductwork that carries heated and cooled air through your home. Over years of use, this debris coats the interior walls of the ducts and gets pushed into every room every time your HVAC system runs.

Atlanta’s heavy spring pollen and red-clay dust make this buildup happen faster here than in many other cities. Homes built on crawl spaces — common throughout the metro — are also more prone to moisture finding its way into ductwork, which adds mold risk on top of ordinary dust and debris.

A full service uses negative-pressure vacuum equipment connected to your main trunk line, along with agitation tools that dislodge debris from every branch line and vent, rather than just wiping down what’s visible near the register. The result is noticeably better indoor air quality and steadier airflow through every room.

When Do You Need Air Duct Cleaning?

  • Visible dust blowing from vents — if you can see debris kick up when the system starts, it’s already circulating through the air you breathe.
  • Musty or stale odor when the AC runs — often a sign of moisture buildup or early mold growth inside the ductwork, common during humid summer months.
  • Recent renovation or new construction — drywall dust and insulation debris settle deep into ducts during remodeling work and rarely clear on their own.
  • Pets in the home — pet hair and dander accumulate in ductwork faster than in pet-free homes, especially with shedding breeds.
  • Household allergy or asthma symptoms getting worse indoors — if symptoms improve when you leave the house, your ductwork is worth inspecting.

How Our Air Duct Cleaning Process Works

1. Inspection

We check every accessible vent, the main trunk line, and the air handler for buildup, moisture, and any signs of mold before quoting a final price.

2. Negative-Pressure Vacuum

A truck-mounted or portable HEPA vacuum system connects to the main line, pulling loosened debris out of the ductwork instead of pushing it further in.

3. Agitation & Vent Cleaning

Brushes and air whips dislodge caked-on debris from duct walls and branch lines while every supply and return register is removed and cleaned individually.

4. Sanitizing (if needed)

If moisture or mold was found during inspection, an EPA-registered antimicrobial treatment is applied to the ductwork interior before closing everything back up.

5. Final Walkthrough

Every vent cover is reinstalled and we walk you through what was found, including before-and-after photos from inside the ducts where accessible.

6. Airflow Check

The system is run and airflow is checked at several registers to confirm nothing was left loose or disconnected during the cleaning process.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should air ducts be cleaned in Atlanta?

Most Atlanta homes need air duct cleaning every 3 to 5 years, but homes with pets, recent renovations, or allergy sufferers often benefit from cleaning every 2 years. Georgia’s pollen season, which runs heavy from March through May, pushes extra debris into ductwork faster than in drier climates, so homes near tree cover or with open crawl spaces may need service sooner.

Is air duct cleaning worth it?

It’s worth it when you have a specific trigger — visible dust blowing from vents, a musty smell when the AC kicks on, recent construction dust, rodent activity, or a household member with worsening allergies or asthma. If none of those apply and your system was cleaned within the last few years, you likely don’t need it yet. We’ll always tell you honestly if your ducts look fine.

How much does air duct cleaning cost in Atlanta?

Air duct cleaning in the Atlanta metro typically runs $350 to $650 for an average single-family home with one HVAC system, depending on the number of vents, duct accessibility, and whether mold treatment is needed. Homes with two systems or crawl-space ductwork usually land at the higher end of that range.

Can dirty air ducts make you sick?

Dirty ducts can circulate dust, pet dander, pollen, and in humid conditions, mold spores, which can worsen allergy and asthma symptoms and cause general respiratory irritation. Atlanta’s humidity, especially from June through September, creates conditions where moisture in ductwork can support mold growth if a system has a condensation or drainage issue.

How long does duct cleaning take?

A standard single-system home in Atlanta takes about 2 to 3 hours from setup to final inspection. Homes with two HVAC systems, heavy mold treatment, or difficult attic and crawl-space access can take closer to 4 to 5 hours.

Do you clean dryer vents too?

Yes, dryer vent cleaning is one of our core services and is often bundled with duct cleaning since both involve clearing lint and debris from home ventilation systems. A clogged dryer vent is a leading cause of house fires, so we recommend checking it annually even if your ducts don’t need service yet.
